Financial Model · April 2026

A sustainable engine.

People ask if our revenue model is sustainable. It is. Here is exactly how the money moves, what we need to scale it, and what self-sustaining looks like in three years.

100 Monthly Partners · $50/mo 40 · 40 · 10 · 10 3 Year Runway to Self-Sustaining
01 · How The Money Moves

Two revenue streams. One mission.

Every Kamp is funded through two converging streams: student registration covers the programming cost of the event itself, and student-leader-driven partnership fundraising underwrites leadership development, staff, and mission expansion. The goal at every campus is 100 monthly partners at $50/month.
01

Event Registration

Students pay to go to Kamp. Registration covers the direct programming cost of every event: food, lodging, activities, logistics.

02

Monthly Partners

Each university works toward 100 monthly partners at $50/month. A mandatory fundraising party invites parents, churches, alumni, and friends into sustained partnership.

03

Recurring Revenue

100 partners × $50/mo = $5,000/mo per campus = $60,000/year in recurring, predictable, diversified revenue. Multiplied across every active campus.

02 · The Split

Every dollar, on purpose.

Every dollar raised by a campus is split the same way. Disciplined, transparent, and designed so local fundraising funds local ministry while seeding the rest of the body.

40%
Programming

Funds the Kamps themselves: camping trip, retreat, and year-round rhythm on that campus.

40%
Leadership Development

Funds student-leader training, content, coaching, and the 6-category discipleship framework on that campus.

10%
HQ

Funds headquarters: staff, systems, finance, storytelling, and the national infrastructure that serves every campus.

10%
Missions

Funds planting new Kamps on new campuses. Tithed forward into the next university God is opening.

04 · The Bottleneck

The only missing piece is seed runway.

The Ask
“The engine works. We need fuel to start it at scale.”

40+ universities have asked us to come. The 40/40/10/10 split is already running on every active campus. What we need is seed money to hire our executive leadership team: the people whose full-time job is generating the revenue that continues the work and plants the next Kamps.

05 · Three Year Trajectory

From seeded to self-sustaining.

With seed runway, we project a three-year glidepath to full self-sustainability. By Year 3, every active university is producing enough fundraised funds to support itself, its regional leadership, HQ, and the planting of the next Kamp, all from the same 40/40/10/10 split.

Year 1
Seed the executive team.

Major-gift runway funds the hiring of the HQ executive and development team. Their full-time job is building the revenue engine that funds the rest of the work.

Year 2
Scale monthly partners.

Every active campus works toward the 100 monthly partners at $50/mo target. HQ development team onboards high-net-worth, church, and org partnerships at the national level.

Year 3
Self-sustaining at every level.

All universities produce enough recurring revenue to cover their own programming, leadership development, regional support, HQ share, and new-Kamp missions share. The body funds the body.
