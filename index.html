The Mission

Transforming students' lives by the gospel.

College students step off campus, into creation, and into community. The mission lives in four words.

01

Camping.

Students step off campus and into God's creation—a weekend in the pines, gathered around a fire beneath an open sky, with space to hear Him.

02

Connection.

Strangers become friends in 48 hours. Different majors, different backgrounds, same fire.

03

Community.

Small groups, shared meals, honest conversation. A family in Christ that lasts beyond the weekend.

04

Christ.

The center, the reason, the author of every Kamp. Everything else flows from here.

The Origin

It started with a camping trip called Camp Lope.

A friend group at Grand Canyon University. One weekend in the pines. Out of that grew a ministry that travels to campuses across the country.

Jarod Lovekamp was a GCU student who built the early days of Camp Lope. On September 29, 2020, Jarod went home to be with the Lord. In his memory, Camp Lope became Kamp Love.

The Model

Fall trip. Spring retreat.
Year-round leaders.

24-hour fall camping trip. Students disconnect from the noise and meet Christ in His creation.

Full weekend spring retreat. Deepens community and sends students back to campus rooted in discipleship.

100% student-led. Leaders are equipped on their campus all year to shepherd the next class into the Kamp Love community.
